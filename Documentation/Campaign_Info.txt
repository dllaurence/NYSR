Victor (just a placeholder name) had a pretty good childhood; parents and siblings that loved him, good friends at school. He got good grades, particularly in practical science and engineering, mostly because he was motivated by learning to make gadgets in imitation of his hero Batman. He also took martial arts, again imitating his hero. His only real problem was a sense of insecurity, of not belonging, and that led him to a slowly increasing relationship with a local gang. This started in jr high, but it became serious in high school. His mother was thinking of sending him to a boarding school because he was on his way to being the techie and IT guy for a local gang when he stumbled across the NYSR. He had mixed feelings about joining, given his sense of belonging and obligation to the gang he's slowly becoming more involved with, but the chance to possibly meet Batman was the deciding factor. He's quite loyal, which is going to make him conflicted between family, gang, and NYSR.

There is one other problem: most of the above is simply a lie. He was raised from childhood to be a cat's paw for someone horrible who needs more covert minions in our world. (Maybe something extra-dimensional who needs more influence in our dimension?) In the darkest version, he was actually bred to be powerful, but also obedient to $BBEG. His mind has probably been meddled with to make him the perfect minion, which can be great for making it harder for him to figure out the truth. His gadgets are actually magic, granted by $BBEG and disguised as technology to keep up the appearance. His parents and family are probably not his family at all, but minions assigned to shape his development. Naturally he knows none of this until the Inception Event, when he gains genuine magical ability, including some (probably magical sense of some variety) that lets him see that all is not as he believed. To start with his own powers are unreliable and dangerous, but his potential is very high--ironically, precisely because of $BBEG's meddling. The senses let him know that something is amiss, which starts the dominos tumbling. Magic that isn't granted by $BBEG and thus can't be shut off if he steps out of line is going to cause him a great deal of trouble, but might save him in the end. Or get him killed or worse. Finding out is what and who he is and why is what his arc is about.

* His love of the power magic gives him is part of the hold his puppetmaster has on him. That's going to really hurt later.

* Working on appropriate abilities and existing gang role to better fit Visions academy and the contests system.

* Suffers from nightmares, which explains why his hero is a monster hunter, Blade. His nightmares are real.

* The inception event gives him his own magic for the first time--he's been manipulated to be magic susceptible. He won't know this for a while, because he's relying on the magic he has, but does gain an innate magic sense which will start revealing the truth about his past.

* Learning about how much he's been manipulated and exploited should lead to a second pivotal choice: is he willing to give up all the magic he has in order to free himself from his puppetmaster? In game that means he's literally going to lose the use of all the points he has in magic. He has to want to be free more than he wants the power magic gives him.

* Once he makes that difficult choice, he can start to discover his own abilities. That's where the points end up: at first those abilities are unreliable and even dangerous--but they're his.

* I think he'll end up with a lot of reluctance to use powers like Mind Control and Telepathy because of his background. Hopefully that can be made into a dramatic conflict for him.
